This is a strong hook because it takes the factory/automation fantasy and replaces sterile machinery with a living ecosystem. The interesting part isn't just “Factorio with monsters”, it's that your production line can eat, get tired, evolve, breed, fight, and misbehave.

Think of it as:

“An ant colony crossed with a factory builder, where every conveyor belt is a creature with needs.”

### Core Pillars

Pillar

### Living Automation

Every worker is a creature, not a machine.

Goblins carry ore, beetles haul loads, slimes clean waste, drakes smelt metal with their breath.

Pillar

### Food Economy

Food replaces electricity.

Workers consume calories while operating. Bigger monsters consume dramatically more.

Pillar

### Evolution Tree

Upgrade by recruiting, breeding, or mutating creatures.

A Goblin line might become Hobgoblin → Goblin Engineer → Goblin Overseer.

Pillar

### Ecosystem Pressure

Starvation, morale, overcrowding, disease, and predators create emergent problems.

A factory can collapse because your mushroom farm failed.

### New Mechanic: Diet Chains

A Salamander Smelter needs charcoal.

Charcoal requires wood.

Wood requires treants or lumber goblins.

So your smelting industry indirectly depends on forestry and food production.

### A Strong 10-Minute Prototype

### If I were prototyping this first

Keep scope tiny

* 3 creatures: Goblin Miner, Goblin Carrier, Goblin Cook

* 3 resources: Ore, Mushrooms, Food

* 1 building: Mushroom Farm

* 1 threat: Hunger

* 1 upgrade: Beetle Hauler

### Win condition

Maintain 100 food surplus while producing 50 processed ore.

### If that feels fun

Then add species, breeding, combat, and evolution.

### The strongest design insight

Key differentiator

Treat food exactly like electricity:

* Every creature has an upkeep draw.

* Farms are your generators.

* Cookhouses are your power plants.

* Food storage is your battery.

* Famine is a brownout.

* Starvation is a blackout.

That single rule gives the game a clear identity and creates interesting decisions at every stage of progression.

A working title that captures the vibe could be Monsterworks, The Hungry Forge, Goblin Warren, or Biofoundry.
